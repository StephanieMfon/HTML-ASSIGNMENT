Classic Plain Nigerian Cocunut Rice

Did you know?

Coconuts are especially high in manganese, which is essential for bone health and the metabolism of carbohydrates, proteins, and cholesterol.

The plain coconut rice is a make-ahead rice dish with plain cocunut flavours, it can be served alongside chicken, overripe fried plantain(dodo) or coleslaw. I personally like it with all three.

Ingredients for Cocunut rice

  • rice   1 cup
  • coconut milk   15 ounce
  • diced tomatoes   (29 ounce)
  • habanero peppers  4 medium
  • green pepper   1 small sized
  • yellow onion   1 small sized
  • vegetable oil   1⁄4 cup
  • sea salt   1 teaspoon
  • black pepper   1 teaspoon

Preparing the rice

  • Boil the rice in water for about 10-15 minutes (enough were it's not too soft because we will be cooking it some more).
  • Heat the vegetable oil in a cooking pot.
  • Add the onions and stir with black pepper for one minute.
  • Add diced tomatoes, habaneros, and coconut milk with the sea salt and cover to cook for about 7 minutes (or when the mixture comes to a boil).
  • Add the rice and stir and cover for about 7 minutes (or when the rice is almost dry).
  • Add the green peppers and let simmer until the rice has absorbed all the juices.
  • Enjoy!
  • note feel free to add more salt and pepper to your taste

Ingredients for fried chicken

  • Chicken meat   1kg
  • Maggi cubes   2 bouillon
  • onion, finely sliced   1 bulb
  • garlic, grated   3 cloves
  • Thyme leaves (Fresh or dried)   1 teaspoon
  • Chili pepper   ½ teaspoon
  • white or black pepper (or both)   1 teaspoon
  • curry powder   ½ teaspoon
  • grated ginger   1 teaspoon
  • water   ½ cup
  • Oil for deep frying
  • Salt to taste

Preparing the Chicken

  • Firstly, add the chicken to a pot with all the spices and seasonings (pepper, curry, onions, garlic, ginger, bouillon cube, etc.)
  • Secondly, add the water and salt, and then bring it to a boil for about 15-20 minutes stirring occasionally.
  • Thirdly, remove the chicken and set it aside after 20 minutes.
  • Lastly, heat the vegetable oil on medium heat and fry the chicken until golden brown.
  • Then, drain the chicken with paper towels. Serve immediately and enjoy with fried rice, sauce/stew alone, or with any preference of food choice.
